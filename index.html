Tiger

The tiger is a large, carnivorous feline species that is native to Asia. They are one of the most recognizable and well-known animals in the world, known for their distinctive orange fur with black stripes. There are several subspecies of tigers, including the Bengal tiger, Siberian tiger, Sumatran tiger, and more. They are all endangered due to habitat loss and poaching for their fur, bones, and other body parts that are used in traditional medicine.

Peacock

The peacock is a beautiful and colorful bird that belongs to the pheasant family. The male peacock is known for its impressive and striking appearance, with a long, iridescent blue-green tail that is decorated with colorful "eye" spots. These tail feathers can reach up to 6 feet in length and are used during courtship displays to attract females. Crocodile

The crocodile is a large, predatory reptile that is found in many parts of the world, including Africa, Australia, Asia, and the Americas. Crocodiles are generally long-lived and can live for up to 70 years. They are also among the largest reptiles in the world, with some species growing up to 23 feet in length and weighing over a ton. They have a tough, scaly skin that helps protect them from injury and also aids in swimming. Crocodiles are carnivorous and feed on a variety of animals.

Penguin

The penguin is a flightless bird that is primarily found in the southern hemisphere, particularly in Antarctica, but also in South Africa, New Zealand, Australia, and South America. Penguins are known for their distinctive black and white coloring, their waddling gait, and their ability to swim. Penguins are adapted to living in cold environments and have several features that help them survive in these harsh conditions. They have a thick layer of insulating feathers that keeps them warm.
